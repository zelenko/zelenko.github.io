Technologies prefered for Web, ERP, and Customer solutions

Here are some technologies, tools, and best practices I observed over the years, and absorbed them for implementation of Web, ERP, and Customer solutions. Some of these are better than others.

Text Editors

Years ago I used Macromedia Dreamweaver, then Notepad++, now its Visual Studio Code.

Also on my radar is Webstorm, Sublime Text, and Vim.

Operating Systems

Now its Windows 10, Windows Server, Red Hat Linux, Debian Linux, Ubuntu, and Amazon Machine Images (AMI).

Years ago I started with Windows 95, then migrated to Windows 98, ME, and NT.

CMS & eCommerce

Wordpress, Joomla, OpenCart, Magento, WooCommerce

Drupal, eZ Publish, osCommerce

Payment Processing

Paypal, Authorize.net, Amazon Pay, Google Wallet

Avalara for tax procesing

Databases

MongoDB, Rocket U2 UniVerse, MySQL, Micosoft SQL

NoSQL is prefered, especially MongoDB. It is written in Golang, and it is fast.

Help Desk System

Dell KACE K1000 Systems, ZenDesk, ManageEngine ServiceDesk Plus

You have to offer support to your systems, right.

ERP Systems

Eclipse ERP, SAP, Microsoft Dynamics, Prophet 21, Openbravo, Covestone

Eclipse ERP is the best ERP for industrial distribution companies in HVAC and PVF industries.

Domain Registration

Godddy, Network Solutions

Get a domain that most people can type without asking how it's spelled.

Cloud

Amazon Elastic Compute Cloud (Amazon EC2), Google App Engine, Rackspace, Digital Ocean

TurnkeyLinux appliances allow for fast installations.

Web Servers

Preference is for Nginx or Lighttpd, because Apache is slower than the other two.

Golang can serve its own, without the need for Nginx or Lighttpd.

Development

PHP, Golang, Node.JS, JavaScript

Golang is fast.
